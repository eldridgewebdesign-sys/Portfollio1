A quick guide

How spreadsheets work

A spreadsheet connection turns your website into your business's filing cabinet. Instead of information living in someone's inbox or a notebook, everything your site collects and manages is kept in a clean, organized table — just like the spreadsheets you already know. No formulas or technical setup required from you.

What a spreadsheet can hold

Think of each spreadsheet as one list of things you care about. Every new entry becomes a row, and every detail about it becomes a column. Your website reads from and writes to that list automatically, so your records stay up to date without any copy-and-paste.

Leads & enquiries

Everyone who contacts you, with their name, message, and date — ready to follow up.

Orders & sales

What was bought, by whom, for how much, and whether it has shipped.

Bookings & appointments

Dates, times, and customer details for anything people can reserve.

Form submissions

Every quote request, sign-up, or contact form neatly collected in one place.

Inventory & products

Items you sell, their prices, and how many you have left in stock.

Client & member info

Details about the people you work with, kept tidy and easy to search.

How it comes together

  1. You tell me what information you want to keep track of.
  2. I set up a spreadsheet with the right columns for it, connected straight to your website.
  3. As people use your site, entries are added to the spreadsheet on their own.
  4. You can view, edit, import, export, or sort that information whenever you like — no technical work needed.

In short: if it's the kind of thing you'd normally track in a list, a spreadsheet connection can store and manage it for you — quietly, in the background, while your website does the work.
