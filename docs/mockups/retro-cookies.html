02The Story

It started with one recipe and a full oven.

It began the way most good things in a kitchen do — with one recipe, written on the back of an envelope, and an oven that ran a little hot. The first batch was for friends. The second batch was because they asked again.

Word got around the way it does about good cookies. People wanted a dozen, then two, then a box for the office. So we started baking on purpose — same recipe, same real butter, same stubborn refusal to rush a cookie out of the oven before it's ready.

We're still small on purpose. Small enough to bake your order the morning you want it, and to remember that you like the edges a little crisp. We'd rather be the cookies you bring to the party than the ones that sat in a bag all week.

“Real butter. No shortcuts.”

— the only rule, since the first batch
